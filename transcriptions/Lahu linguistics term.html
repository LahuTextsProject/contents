A single phoneme may have several allophones.

fɨ̀-khɔ-pɛ̀-khɔ̂ tê khɔ̂ tí ɔ̀-qhɔ lo ɔ̀-šə́-pa-khɔ̂ láy khɔ̂ cɔ̀ g̈a ve yò.

The relationship among the allophones of a phoneme is called complementary distribution.

ɔ̀-šə́ pa khɔ̂ chi tê-phā tê khɔ̂ lɛ tê khɔ̂ cá dàʔ ve ɔ̀-qhâ thàʔ chɛ̂ kɨ̀ pa g̈a ve šū-dàʔ-khɔ̂ phɛ̀ʔ ve tɛ̀ʔ qôʔ ve yò.

All verbs in Lahu can be negated by mâ.

Lâhū khɔ̂ te-tɔ̂ phɛ̀ʔ ve qo, mâ-hêʔ-tɔ̂ phɛ̀ʔ ve "mâ" thàʔ yù tɛ̂ʔ ni g̈a ve yò.

negative mâ-hêʔ-tɔ̂

morpheme tɔ̂-hòʔ

"ó-qō-šī" qôʔ ve tɔ̂-khɔ̂ ɔ̀-qhɔ lo tɔ̂-hòʔ šɛ̂ʔ cə̀ cɔ̀ ve yò.

The word "ó-qō-šī" contains three morphemes.

classifier ɔ̀-cə̀-pɛ̀-tɔ̂

part of speech tɔ̂-cə̀

noun particle tɔ̂-mɛ-jûʔ-pā

verb particle te-khɔ̂-jûʔ-pā

unrestricted particle tɔ̂-jûʔ-mâ-pā

non-final unrestricted particle tɔ̂-kā-jûʔ-mâ-pā

final unrestricted particle tɔ̂-mɛ̄-jûʔ-mâ-pā (tɔ̂-lɛ-jûʔ-mâ-pā)
